4.1.2 Visualisation of the Country Coefficients

random_effects <- ranef(nullmodell)
random_effects_df <- as.data.frame(random_effects)
ggplot(random_effects_df, aes(y = grp, x = condval, color = condval > 0)) +
  geom_point() +
  geom_vline(xintercept = 0, linetype = "dashed", color = "black") +
  scale_color_manual(values = c("red", "blue")) +
  labs(y = "Country", x = "Coeff") +
  xlim(c(-0.6, 0.6)) +
  guides(color = F)

The following graph illustrates the effects of the null model on different countries. When evaluating injustice, citizens in the Czech Republic, Norway, and Portugal have the least faith in justice, as indicated by their relatively high positive effects in the country comparison. Conversely, participants from the Netherlands, Austria, and Italy demonstrate the strongest negative effects in the model, suggesting a stronger belief in justice.

The second model includes the effects of belonging to a discriminated group (dscrgrp), having trust in the legal justice system (trstlgl), and believing that citizens have a fair chance to achieve the level of education they seek (evfredu). All independent variables are fixed in the model, and the deviance decreases compared to the null model. All the effects are statistically significant. Belonging to a discriminated group has a positive effect, while trust in legal institutions and belief in educational fairness have negative effects.
